Executive Summary

PredictFi is a fully on-chain, non-custodial prediction market protocol deployed on BNB Smart Chain (BSC). It enables anyone to trade outcome shares (YES / NO) on real-world events using BNB as collateral, with instant payouts settled directly by the smart contract — no intermediary, no delay, no trust required.

Trustless Settlement
No human intermediary decides who wins — the contract enforces every payout automatically.
Transparent Odds
All pool sizes are on-chain and visible to every participant at all times.
PRFI Incentives
Utility and governance token rewarding accurate predictors, stakers, and active community members.
Zero Withdrawal Delays
Winners claim BNB immediately after market resolution — no waiting periods.

How PredictFi Works

High-Level Flow

1. Market CreationAdmin creates a market with a question, event outcomes, and a deadline (endTime).
2. Market DiscoveryUsers browse open markets and view live odds and pool sizes on each event card.
3. Prediction PlacementUsers place a YES or NO prediction by sending BNB to the contract before endTime.
4. Market ClosesNo new predictions are accepted after the endTime timestamp passes.
5. ResolutionAdmin resolves each event with the verified real-world result (YES or NO).
6. PayoutWinners call claimWinnings() and receive BNB instantly from the contract.

Participants

RoleDescription
Admin (Owner)Creates markets, sets event names, resolves outcomes. Currently the PredictFi team; governance transitions to PRFI holders post-mainnet.
PredictorsAny wallet that sends BNB to place a YES or NO position before closing time.
WinnersPredictors whose chosen outcome matches the resolved result — receive stake back plus proportional share of the losing pool.
LosersPredictors on the wrong side. Their BNB is redistributed to winners minus the platform fee.

Market Architecture

Market Structure

FieldTypeDescription
iduint256Auto-incrementing unique market identifier
questionstringThe headline question, e.g. "FIFA World Cup 2026 — Who wins?"
endTimeuint256UNIX timestamp after which no new predictions are accepted
eventCountuint256Number of sub-events (1–50) within the market

Market Events (Sub-Outcomes)

FieldTypeDescription
iduint256Event index (1-based)
namestringEvent label, e.g. "Brazil wins", "Spain wins"
resolvedboolWhether the admin has settled this event
resultOutcomeNONE (default), YES, or NO
yesPooluint256Total BNB staked on YES (in wei)
noPooluint256Total BNB staked on NO (in wei)
totalPooluint256yesPool + noPool

User Positions

Each user can place multiple positions on the same event. All positions are stored as an array and aggregated for display and payout. The contract tracks per-position: choice (YES/NO), amount (BNB sent), and claimed status.

Multi-Event Markets

For markets with multiple events (e.g. a tournament bracket), each event is independently priced and resolved. Users can predict on one, some, or all events within a market.

05

Prediction Mechanics

Placing a Prediction

A user calls predict(marketId, eventId, choice) and attaches BNB as msg.value.

On-Chain Validation Rules

  • 01Market must exist (market.id != 0)
  • 02Event must be within range: 1 ≤ eventId ≤ eventCount
  • 03Current timestamp must be before market.endTime
  • 04Event must not already be resolved
  • 05Choice must be YES (1) or NO (2)
  • 06msg.value must be greater than 0

    • Implied Probability / Live Odds

    The implied probability updates in real-time as predictions are placed. The UI displays these as percentage bars on each market card.

    P(YES) = yesPool / totalPool
    Implied probability of YES outcome
    P(NO) = noPool / totalPool
    Implied probability of NO outcome

    Winner Calculation & Payout Formula

    Core Model

    PredictFi uses a parimutuel-style settlement model. There is no house liquidity — the losers' pool is entirely redistributed to winners, minus the platform fee.

    Step-by-Step Calculation

    // Step 1 — Define pools
winningPool = pool of the correct side
              (yesPool if R=YES, noPool if R=NO)
losingPool  = pool of the incorrect side
              (noPool if R=YES, yesPool if R=NO)

// Step 2 — User's winning amount
winningAmount = sum of all the user's correct positions
    userLosingShare = (losingPool × winningAmount) / winningPool
    Step 3 — Proportional share of the losing pool
    fee = (userLosingShare × platformFee) / 100
    Step 4 — Platform fee applied only to profit, never original stake
    winnings = winningAmount + userLosingShare − fee
    Step 5 — Final payout to winner

    Example Walkthrough

    Suppose a market resolves as YES with the following state:

    Example — Market Resolved as YES
    yesPool10 BNB
    noPool30 BNB
    totalPool40 BNB
    User staked on YES2 BNB
    userLosingShare = (30 × 2) / 106 BNB
    fee = (6 × 5) / 1000.3 BNB
    winnings = 2 + 6 − 0.37.7 BNB
    Net profit on 2 BNB stake+5.7 BNB (+285%)

    Fee Design Proof

    Σ fee(i) = (platformFee × losingPool) / 100
    Total fees always equal exactly 5% of the losing pool — the contract never runs into a shortfall.

    Edge Cases

    ScenarioBehaviour
    User predicted the losing sideclaimWinnings reverts with "You lost"
    All stakes are on one sideOther pool is 0; users get stake back; fee = 0
    User has already claimedTransaction reverts with "Already claimed"
    Market not yet resolvedTransaction reverts with "Not resolved yet"

    Security Properties

    Non-Custodial

    BNB is held in the contract and paid out directly to users — no intermediary wallet ever holds user funds.

    No Reentrancy Risk

    Claim logic marks positions as claimed before calling transfer(), following the checks-effects-interactions pattern.

    Immutable Pool Math

    Once a market closes, pool sizes are final. Under Solidity 0.8.x checked arithmetic, overflow is impossible. No oracle manipulation risk on settlement calculations.

    Fee Structure

    Fee TypeRateApplied To
    Platform Fee5%Applied only to the loser-pool share redistributed to winners — never to the original stake
    Withdrawal Fee0%No fee for claiming winnings from the contract
    Listing Fee0%Free for all approved market listings
    Key Point

    The 5% platform fee is only applied to the profit portion — the loser-pool share. A winner always recovers their full original stake. Total fees collected always equal exactly 5% of the losing pool — mathematically guaranteed (see §6).

    There are no hidden fees, no withdrawal delays, and no spread.

    Security & Audits

    Current Status

    The smart contract is deployed on BSC Testnet for community testing. A formal security audit by a top-tier firm is scheduled for Q3 2026 prior to mainnet launch.

    Security Design Principles

  • 01
    Claim-Before-Transfer — positions are marked as claimed before any transfer() call, preventing reentrancy attacks.
  • 02
    Input Validation — all function inputs are validated on-chain with explicit require statements.
  • 03
    Owner-Gated Resolution — market resolution is restricted to the contract owner; cannot be triggered by predictors.
  • 04
    Fixed-Supply Math — payout calculations are deterministic and overflow-safe under Solidity 0.8.x checked arithmetic.
  • 05
    No Flash Loan Risk — prediction amounts are locked until market close; pool values cannot be manipulated within a single transaction.
    • Responsible Disclosure

    If you discover a vulnerability, contact security@predictfi.io before public disclosure. Responsible disclosures are eligible for PRFI bug bounty rewards.
